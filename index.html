The Bride

Yvonne is the older So Sistah, and was born and raised in Houston, TX by the most (for)giving parents ever. Growing up, Yvonne had a gazillion hobbies including piano, violin, tennis, dance, Chinese school, math classes, etc. and she wants her parents to know the eternal appreciation she feels for letting their children rob them of their youth.

Yvonne has always been infinitely curious, constantly asking why or seeking answers. Yvonne is quite literally the polar opposite of Nils. She’s still stumped as to why he loves her so much.

The Groom

Nils is the eldest of the three half-German and half-Vietnamese Schimmelmann siblings. He was born in Los Angeles and raised in San Diego where he developed a love for the outdoors and strived to become a junior ranger at every National Park he visited. At age 9, the Schimmelmann family moved to Indiana and Nils started to consider himself a midwestern boy as he joined the Boy Scouts and spent more time playing in the local forests and creeks. Nils also took an interest in computers, checking out books from the local library to program his own games, and building his own PC to play them. This hobby would later lead to his BS in Computer Science and MS in Bioinformatics and bring him back to California. After graduation, Nils moved back to to the Bay Area to work in Silicon Valley. In the Bay, he expanded his interests to include snowboarding, rock climbing, and weight training at the local gym.

HIS VERSION

I met Yvonne through mutual friends at the local gym. Yvonne initially thought I was obnoxious and did not think much of me until my birthday party where she learned that I loved snowboarding.

After meeting over bubble tea, she still thought I was obnoxious, and I thought she was extremely negative. Luckily, these short sighted problems went away and we would later go out on many “events” because she did not want to think we were going on actual dates! Fast forward 6 months later, Yvonne finally agreed to be "really dating" and I got upgraded to boyfriend.

On Black Friday, I suggested we go hang out in SF and walk up the hills to burn off some of the turkey we ate. Yvonne pretty much likes anything that requires physical activity so she just thought we were going on a hike. We walked to the Ferry Building, then up to the top of the Coit Tower, where I then got down on one knee to propose to Yvonne while she was taking pictures with her phone. Yvonne was completely shocked and said “yes!”

Frequently Asked Questions


  • "What is the parking situation like?"

    There is plenty of parking at the clubhouse! Not only is it plentiful, but the parking spaces are actually large enough to fit your vehicle!

  • "Do I need to rent a car?"

    If you plan on staying for a few more days or plan on visiting some of the national parks or go up to see some snow in Tahoe, renting a car is MOST DEFINITELY recommended. Otherwise, as mentioned before, Uber and Lyft are very popular and reliable here in the bay area.

  • "Can I bring my kids?"

    Quite a few of our friends and family are proud new parents! Some of you mentioned hesitation around bringing your kids. Our answer is, you know your kids best, what their needs are, and how they will behave at such a function. Whatever you decide, we support you! We just ask that you let us know far in advance so we can estimate guest count (and high chairs, if needed).
