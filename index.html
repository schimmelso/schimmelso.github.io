The Groom

Nils is the eldest of the three half-German and half-Vietnamese Schimmelmann siblings. He was born in Los Angeles and raised in San Diego where he developed a love for the outdoors and strived to become a junior ranger at every National Park he visited. At age 9, the Schimmelmann family moved to Indiana and Nils started to consider himself a midwestern boy as he joined the Boy Scouts and spent more time playing in the local forests and creeks. Accommodations

Here are some recommended accommodations from our venue. We have room blocks at each of them, but we also recommend looking into Airbnb and VRBO, especially if you have a large party or are staying for a while. If you prefer to stay in San Francisco, the BART ride (public transit train) to Walnut Creek is about 35-45 min from downtown SF. Uber and Lyft are also the most popular options (often with discounts on your first ride) for getting around if you will not have a car.

Frequently Asked Questions


  • "What is the parking situation like?"

    There is plenty of parking at the clubhouse! Not only is it plentiful, but the parking spaces are actually large enough to fit your vehicle!

  • "Do I need to rent a car?"

    If you plan on staying for a few more days or plan on visiting some of the national parks or go up to see some snow in Tahoe, renting a car is MOST DEFINITELY recommended. Otherwise, as mentioned before, Uber and Lyft are very popular and reliable here in the bay area.

  • "Where are you registered?"

    Nowhere! We do not have a lot of storage space, so we are forgoing the registry tradition. Instead, we suggest gifts in the form of Red Envelopes, a traditional Chinese gift given at weddings to wish the couple well as they embark on their new life together. Actual red envelopes are optional as greeting cards or other envelopes work wonderfully as well. We would also be more than happy to donate to your favorite charity in our name! All that said, the best gift we could ever receive is your presence at our wedding!
